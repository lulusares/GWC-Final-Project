AP study tips and resources

AP's are stressful, here are some resources to help to prepare yourself!

Social Studies

AP World History
AP U.S History
AP Psychology
AP Government
AP Economics

Social Studies AP's usually include essays, so make sure that you take your time. When you're

finished writing your essay, remember to ensure that you've covered all the requirements. There are

usually a lot of MC questions and not a lot of time so even though you should manage your time,

make sure that you read the whole question and give yourself a second to think of the correct answer.

Don't rush!

Science AP's usually have two sections; multiple choiceFRQs (Free Response Question)

One thing to take note of is the in addition to multiple choice questions and FRQs, on the AP Biology

exam, there are also Grid-ins, where you bubble in a numerical answer. The general advice is

to do the grid-ins first rather than the multiple choice since you can guess on MC questions but not

necessarily on grid-ins. Another important thing to remember is that for the FRQs, there are questions

that have different lengths and different point values, so make sure you organize your time such that

you take the 'importance' of the question into consideration. In terms of studying, the best method

generally to take a number of practice questions in areas that you aren't confident in in order to

familiarize yourself with the question styles

AP English Literature and Composition

English , the most self explanatory AP there is ---- or, maybe not

The format for the Language and Composition exam is around 55 multiple choice questions

followed by 3 essays. As with the previous exams, practice is key, especially with the reading

passages because you really never know what the College Board will throw at you; sometimes they

give interesting and relevant articles, other times they can give studies from the 1800s

that no one really cares about. One tip for the essays though, they tell you to write the

synthesis and argumentative essay within the same time period, so make sure you manage your time

correctly. In addition, if you can't think of any outside evidence for the argument essay, you

can make up studies that prove your point as long as they sound plausible

(no one is really gonna check lol). The format and content for Literature is slightly different.

Spanish Literature and Culture

Foreign Language! Very very fun stuff

The test generally consists of MC Reading passages, MC Reading + Listening passages,

MC Listening passages, email writing, persuasive essay, and two speaking presentations.

The speaking section is generally going to be a recording of your responses to a conversation

played from a pre-recorded tape in addition to a cultural presentation, which is basically a

minute monologue on a topic provided by the college board

Again, the best way to study for these exams is to practice a lot! Find reading and listening

passages online, or ask your teacher for any recordings that they may have
